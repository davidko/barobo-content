Lesson 4

Getting the LED to change color based on a set amount of time, like a traffic light.

Objective

In this lesson you will learn how a traffic signal micro processor is programmed. You will control the amount of time the light stays yellow based on the posted speed limit and the accepted speed limit, that is, the speed the traffic actually moves. According to highway and safety engineers the safe stopping rate for an automobile is 15 feet/second. Adding reaction time of about 1.5 seconds to decide you are going to stop, at 30 mph (44 feet/second) it would take 4 seconds to safely stop a car. A simplified formula is stop_time=(1.467*MPH)/15+1.5. Design a traffic light that safely allows a car to stop in a 30 MPH zone.

import barobo
dongle = barobo.Dongle()
dongle.connect()
robot = dongle.getLinkbot('9418')
import time
mph=float(input("mph="))
print(mph)
yellow_time=(1.467*mph/15.0+1.5)
print(yellow_time)
time.sleep(3.0)
robot.setLEDColor(0,255,0)
time.sleep(3)
robot.setLEDColor(255,255,0)
time.sleep(yellow_time)
robot.setLEDColor(255,0,0)
